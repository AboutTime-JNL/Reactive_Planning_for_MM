Reactive Configuration Space Collision-Free Motion Planning for Mobile Manipulator in Unforeseen Scenarios

Ninglong Jin, Guangbao Zhao, Jianhua Wu*, Zhenhua Xiong
Shanghai Jiao Tong University
*Indicates Corresponding Author
Code

Abstract

This paper investigates reactive collision-free planning for mobile manipulator (MM) in unknown, cluttered environments where real-time perception data guides local planning. In these environments, non-convex obstacles limit the effectiveness of optimization-based methods due to local minima. While sampling-based methods can avoid local minima, they often fail to achieve the high frequencies needed for reactive planning due to high collision detection costs, particularly in configuration space (C-space) with dynamic obstacles represented by pointcloud. To address these issues, we propose a hierarchical planning architecture in C-space. This approach dynamically adjust the perception range based on the motion trends of the MM, limiting invalid areas to enhance the frequency of collision information updates. For the mobile base, we introduce a tangential velocity optimization to provide heuristic guidance for whole-body planning, helping the system avoid local minima. We also apply a control post-processing method to refine the base's path and reduce oscillations. This framework was tested on a 9-degree-of-freedom mobile manipulator. Simulations in various scenarios show that the proposed method is robust, achieving around 80% success rates even with dynamic obstacles. Real-world tests further demonstrate its effectiveness and practical applicability.

Framework

Hierarchical sampling-based MPC planning framework. The ESDF thread supplies collision information for planning. The MB planning uses obstacle tangential MPPI with dynamic system, generating a path P via a post-processing algorithm. The whole-body planning computes collision-free U, tracks P when local minima are encountered, and feeds results back to the ESDF thread to adjust the update range.
